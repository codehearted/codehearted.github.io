The Express Area provides quick access for young adults and adults to check their email, get some information from the web, access Cliolink 2001 and all of it's associated resources, get after-hours access to various City-related services and forms, as well as catch up on recent magazines, newspapers, and other periodicals. It is adjacent to the entrance to the museum, and as such, it contains an interactive learning center providing information about the museum and it's exhibits.

A single (1) City-Hall Station and a (1) Public B&W Printer dot the far west side of the Express Area. Here, citizens can print out government forms, as well as access various civic information.

Moving right, there is a cluster of sixteen (16) Time-Limited Public Workstations. This cluster has access to the full range of Cliolink services, but will be limited to short-term use to allow as many people as possible to use these machines.

A single (1) Museum-themed Interactive Learning Station is located near the northeast corner of this area.

The Shops are located directly west of this area. North, across the main Lobby, is the Young Adult Area.
